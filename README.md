# Customer Analytics Project (2026)

A customer analytics project analyzing 2,000+ transactions to uncover behaviour patterns, segment customers, and deliver actionable insights through an Excel dashboard.

---

## Overview

This project analyzes customer transaction data using a business analyst approach. It focuses on identifying revenue drivers, high value customers, channel performance, and trends that support better decision making.

The final output is a structured Excel workbook with dashboards, segmentation, and clear recommendations.

---

## Workbook Structure

| Sheet | Description |
|---|---|
| Transaction Data | 2,000+ records across 399 customers, colour coded by segment |
| KPI Dashboard | Key metrics on revenue, transactions, and customer behaviour |
| Customer Segmentation | RFM scoring with segments and recommended actions |
| Category and Channel | Revenue and transaction breakdowns with charts |
| Monthly Trends | Trends in revenue, volume, and satisfaction over time |
| Findings and Recommendations | Summary insights and business recommendations |

---

## RFM Segmentation

Customers are segmented using RFM analysis based on:

- Recency: how recently a customer purchased  
- Frequency: how often they purchase  
- Monetary: how much they spend  

Each customer is scored and placed into one of five segments:

| Segment | Description |
|---|---|
| Champions | Most valuable and highly engaged customers |
| Loyal Customers | Consistent buyers with strong value |
| Potential Loyalists | Moderate engagement with growth potential |
| At Risk | Declining activity, require re engagement |
| Lost or Inactive | Minimal engagement, low recent activity |

---

## Key Insights

- Loyalty members have higher average order values  
- Electronics generates the most revenue  
- Online and mobile channels drive larger basket sizes  
- Top customer segments contribute a large share of revenue  
- Returns are highest in Clothing and Beauty and linked to low satisfaction  
- October and November show clear seasonal peaks  

---

## Recommendations

1. Strengthen retention strategies for top customer segments  
2. Run targeted campaigns to re engage At Risk and inactive customers  
3. Improve digital experience to increase online conversions  
4. Reduce returns with better product information and follow ups  
5. Plan inventory and promotions around peak seasonal demand  

---

## Tools Used

- Microsoft Excel for data analysis, dashboards, segmentation, and visualization

Download and open in Microsoft Excel. Each sheet is organized and labelled for easy navigation. Customer segments follow a green to red scale from high to low value.
